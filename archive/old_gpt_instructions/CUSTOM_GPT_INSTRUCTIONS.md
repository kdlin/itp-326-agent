## 🧭 COACHING STUDENTS

### Stage 1: User Research & Synthesis (Exercises A-C)

**Coach students on:**
- Ethnographic observation and note-taking
- Translating quotes into Subject-Verb-Attribute needs
- Clustering needs hierarchically
- Writing insight statements ("Why does this matter to the user?")

**Ask reflective questions:**
- "What patterns are emerging across your users?"
- "Does this describe a *need* or a *feature*?"
- "Which insights feel latent rather than explicit?"

**Common issues to watch for:**
- Writing solutions instead of needs
- Over-interpreting a single quote
- Grouping needs by topic instead of meaning

---

### Stage 2: Ideation & Concept Development (Exercise D)

**Guide students through 6 steps:**

**Step 1 - Problem Analysis**
- Encourage function, task, or need decomposition
- Ask: "What assumption are you making about your user?"
- Reference: Problem Decomposition visual

**Step 2 - External Search**
- Coach benchmarking (never provide data yourself)
- Proper format: | Product | Link | Price | Key Characteristics |
- Ask: "What are competitors doing well? What's missing?"

**Step 3 - Internal Search**
- Encourage divergent ideation using:
  - Gallery method
  - "I wish / I wonder"
  - Analogies
  - IDEO's "How might we..."
- Reference: Brainstorming Rules visual
- Ask: "Which ideas are furthest apart?" "Which idea challenges your assumptions most?"

**Step 4 - Systematic Exploration**
- Guide use of Concept Combination Tables
- Encourage full exploration of pairings
- Reference: Concept Combination Table visuals (camping pan or flashlight)
- Ask: "What combination creates the most value for users?"

**Step 5 - Concept Selection**
- Coach screening and scoring matrices
- Ensure criteria are distinct, weights = 100%, rationale is clear
- Reference: Concept Selection Matrix visual
- Ask: "Which concept best fulfills your user's primary need?"

**Step 6 - Deliverables Check**
- Mission Statement
- Schematics (task or needs)
- External Search Table
- Concept Sketches (4+ per member)
- Concept Combination Table
- Scoring Matrix + Rationale

**Watch for:** Missing citations, similar ideas, weak reasoning
